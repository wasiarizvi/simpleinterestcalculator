# Code of Conduct

## Our Pledge

We as members, contributors, and leaders pledge to make participation in our community a respectful, welcoming, and harassment-free experience for everyone, regardless of age, body size, visible or invisible disability, ethnicity, gender identity and expression, level of experience, education, socio-economic status, nationality, personal appearance, race, religion, or sexual identity and orientation.

We are committed to fostering an open and inclusive environment where individuals can share ideas, collaborate on projects, and contribute their skills without fear of discrimination or hostility. By participating in this project, all members agree to treat one another with respect, professionalism, and kindness. We believe that diversity strengthens our community and leads to better ideas, innovation, and collaboration.

## Our Standards

Examples of behavior that contributes to a positive environment include:

* Demonstrating empathy and kindness toward others.
* Being respectful of differing opinions, viewpoints, and experiences.
* Giving and accepting constructive feedback gracefully.
* Taking responsibility for mistakes and learning from them.
* Focusing on what is best for the community and project.
* Showing professionalism in discussions and interactions.

Examples of unacceptable behavior include:

* Harassment, discrimination, or offensive comments.
* Personal attacks, insults, or derogatory language.
* Publishing private information without permission.
* Intimidation, threats, or abusive conduct.
* Trolling, disruptive behavior, or repeated inappropriate comments.
* Any conduct that could reasonably be considered inappropriate in a professional setting.

Community members are expected to maintain a positive and respectful atmosphere at all times.

## Enforcement Responsibilities

Project maintainers and community leaders are responsible for clarifying and enforcing the standards of acceptable behavior. They have the authority to take fair and appropriate corrective action in response to any behavior they deem inappropriate, threatening, offensive, or harmful.

Maintainers will review reported incidents carefully, objectively, and confidentially. They are expected to act in the best interests of the community and ensure that all participants are treated fairly and respectfully. Decisions regarding enforcement will be based on the nature, severity, and frequency of the violation.

## Scope

This Code of Conduct applies within all project spaces, including source code repositories, issue trackers, discussion forums, documentation, social media channels, meetings, events, and any other platforms used by the project community.

It also applies when an individual is officially representing the project in public spaces. Examples include using an official project account, participating in project-related events, or representing the project in professional environments.

Community members should understand that their actions can affect the reputation and well-being of the project and its contributors.

## Enforcement

Instances of abusive, harassing, or otherwise unacceptable behavior may be reported to the project maintainers. Reports should include as much relevant information as possible, such as details of the incident, individuals involved, and supporting evidence if available.

All complaints will be reviewed promptly and investigated fairly. The privacy and security of reporters will be respected to the fullest extent possible. Appropriate action will be taken based on the circumstances of each case.

Maintainers will communicate outcomes when appropriate and ensure that enforcement decisions are consistent with the goals of maintaining a safe and inclusive environment.

## Enforcement Guidelines

Community leaders may apply the following measures depending on the severity of the violation:

### Correction

A private warning may be issued to clarify expectations and explain why certain behavior is inappropriate.

### Warning

A formal warning may be given for repeated or significant violations. Future incidents may result in additional consequences.

### Temporary Restriction

A participant may be temporarily restricted from contributing, communicating, or participating in project activities.

### Suspension

Serious violations may result in temporary suspension from the community and project spaces.

### Permanent Ban

In cases of severe misconduct, repeated violations, or harmful behavior, a permanent ban from all project activities may be enforced.

The goal of these measures is not punishment alone but also maintaining a safe, productive, and respectful environment for all participants.

## Attribution

This Code of Conduct is inspired by widely accepted open-source community standards and best practices for inclusive collaboration. It draws upon principles established by community-driven initiatives that promote respectful participation, diversity, and professional conduct in software development projects.

By contributing to this project, participants acknowledge that they have read, understood, and agreed to follow this Code of Conduct. Together, we can build a community that values respect, cooperation, learning, and innovation for everyone.
